A bit about me

My name is Michael and I am among many other things an information security professional. I've spent most of my career specializing in incident response and threat hunting. I've done quite a bit of detection engineering in recent years as well and I hope I could write quite a bit about all these things some day.

Red-teaming is another pursuit of mine, although I haven't dedicated as much time to it as I would have liked.

Work and careers aside, I've spent a lot of time in recent years practicing and improving my offensive security skills. Knowing about attack techniques and tactics is one thing, developing practical skills executing those attacks is another. I learned that very quickly when I first attempted the OSCP exam. I was reminded of that fact again by the my first attempt at the OSED certification as well (I'm glad to say I've passed both exams now). I have consistently gained skills and knowledge that has helped me become a better security professional by practicing and studying offensive security in general, and I consider such pursuit to be foundations of all technical fields in our industry.

Recently, I wanted to focus less on certifications and formal pursuits and more on passion-projects and research. I have so many ideas for tools, attack-techniques and other proejcts, and that's what I have been focusing on, as free-time permits.


Ater passing my OSED, I wanted to practice exploit development so much, learn and practice vulnerability research and so much more. However, I found myself studying EDRs and how to evade them, learning how they work. That happened to intersect with some of the more advanced exploitation labs and EDR-evasion techniques I've had my eye on, which mostly involve Windows kernel-mode drivers,how to exploit them and how they're used to implement modern EDRs. That as a result is what I've been spending a lot of my time studying in the past few months. I am planning on writing about what I've learned so far and refocusing on developing PoC exploits.


Infosec aside, I am huge fan of sci-fi. Books, movies, shows, I'm all for it!

About this site

I started looking into how to host a blog in 2025. There are endless options but I kept returning to simpler approaches.

Github pages does a great job of out-sourcing the hosting and site-management side of things. But static-site-generators like Jekyll just seem to lack character. Being a personal blog site and all, I saw it fitting to use a format I myself like and appreciate greatly: Simple HTML sites with a very light touch of CSS. Let me know what you think of this site and how I'm doing so far.


Thanks for visiting my blog and stay tuned for more posts from me.
